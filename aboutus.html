Organization Overview

SAWS

ICMHEWS

The South African Weather Service (SAWS) is a Section 3(a) public entity under the Ministry of Environmental Affairs, governed by a Board. Established as a public entity on 15 July 2001, under the South African Weather Service Act (No. 8 of 2001) and later amended in 2013, SAWS serves as the authoritative body for weather and climate forecasting in South Africa. As a member of the World Meteorological Organization (WMO), it adheres to international meteorological standards. SAWS is also designated as the Aviation Meteorological Authority by the State, providing essential weather services to the aviation sector, marine operations, and other key industries, while fulfilling international obligations. The organization offers two distinct types of services: public good services funded by the government and paid-for commercial services.

ICMHEWS

From April 16 to May 10, 2024, the South African Weather Service (SAWS) conducted a transformative series of workshops across three districts as part of the Integrated Climate-driven Multi-Hazard Early Warning System (ICMHEWS). This extensive initiative, funded by the Government of Flanders, aligns with the global "Early Warning for All" initiative and aims to strengthen community resilience against climate-induced weather events.

ICMHEWS

The workshops unfolded as follows:

• In the Lejweleputswa District Municipality of the Free State Province, workshops were held from April 16 to April 19, including a significant community event on April 18.
• In the uMgungundlovu District Municipality of KwaZulu-Natal Province, workshops took place from April 22 to April 26, featuring a community event on April 25.
• In the Vhembe District Municipality of Limpopo Province, the series concluded with workshops from May 6 to May 10, and a community event on May 9.
